Text #1

A lonely soul dug into the earth as she stares into the eyes of her loved ones

She sees them as clear as the body of water sitting beside her.

Helping; healing as it comes as her sixth sense.

She dances with the wind, leaves blowing and swaying with her as her spirit strengthens. A lonely soul dug into the earth as she stares into the eyes of her loved ones

She sees them as clear as the body of water sitting beside her.

Helping; healing as it comes as her sixth sense.

She dances with the wind, leaves blowing and swaying with her as her spirit strengthens.

How amazing is it to have ‘she.’ What a charm to come across. A four-leaf clover.

Except she’s a force of nature. As you arrive closer you can notice cuts and scrapes where they attempted to tear her down.

But still she stands strongly and tall buried into her mother earth.

Energy overworking the body as people come to speak to her.

Without saying a word, she can catch an entire glimpse of their life.

Nonetheless they share their deepest thoughts, their most personal and loved creations with her.

She knows exactly how to make someone feel like their body holds the brightest star in the sky.

As the night arrives closer and colder the people pack up their things and get ready to go.

As any energy is failed to be returned back to her she asks herself why can’t anyone see she’s there too.

She pleads to be seen, to be appreciated for the energy she freely gives away.

Is this a charm or just for the people around her?

A curse was born with her, residing in her soul.

As the night grows, she feels even more alone than before.

She’s sick of not being seen, heard or understood

When people came to see her the next day they noticed something different about her. Her leaves were closed instead of spread.

They question her as to why she’s being inconsiderate. As if her containing her energy is an act of selfishness.

“Im here too.”
